Drought

Drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to water scarcity and dry conditions. It results in reduced soil moisture, diminished water sources such as rivers and reservoirs, and impacts agricultural productivity and natural ecosystems. Droughts can have severe economic, social, and environmental consequences, including crop failures, food shortages, water restrictions, and increased risk of wildfires.
