Earthquake

An earthquake is a sudden and violent shaking of the ground caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. This movement releases energy, which propagates in the form of seismic waves, leading to the shaking of the ground. Earthquakes can vary in intensity and duration, and they often result in widespread damage to buildings, infrastructure, and the environment. They can also trigger secondary hazards such as landslides, tsunamis, and aftershocks, posing significant risks to human life and property.
